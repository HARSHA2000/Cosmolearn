## What This FDP Is About

AI tools are changing how educators work. Faculty who learn to use them effectively can cut lesson preparation time by 60%, create more engaging assessments, and stay current with evolving research — all without learning to code.

This FDP is practical and tool-focused. Every session involves direct hands-on use of the tools — not slides about AI, but AI in action. Faculty leave with a personal workflow they can use from the next teaching day.

The program is designed to be accessible to all departments — CS, non-CS, science, arts — because the tools covered require no technical background.

## Curriculum

### Day 1 — Core AI Tools for Teaching (8 hours)

**Module 1: Understanding the AI Landscape for Educators (1.5 hours)**
- What LLMs can and cannot do — setting realistic expectations
- Privacy and data considerations when using AI tools in an institution
- Comparing tools: ChatGPT vs. Claude vs. Gemini for education tasks
- Prompt basics: how to get good outputs from the first try

**Module 2: Lesson Planning and Content Generation (2.5 hours)**
- Generating lesson plans aligned to VTU / AICTE syllabi
- Creating differentiated content for different learner levels
- Writing learning objectives in Bloom's taxonomy format with AI
- Generating analogies, examples, and case studies for abstract concepts
- Hands-on: Each participant builds a complete lesson plan for one unit

**Module 3: Assessment Design and Question Banks (2 hours)**
- Creating MCQs with correct and plausible distractors
- Writing short-answer and case-study questions at different difficulty levels
- Generating rubrics for assignments and project work
- Blooms-mapped question classification
- Hands-on: Build a 20-question mixed-format question bank for a topic of choice

**Module 4: NotebookLM for Research and Course Prep (2 hours)**
- Uploading papers, textbooks, and syllabi
- Asking focused questions across multiple documents
- Generating summaries, timelines, and study guides
- Creating audio overviews for student consumption
- Hands-on: Upload 3 research papers and extract key insights for a lecture

### Day 2 — Presentations, Tools Integration, and Workflow (8 hours)

**Module 5: Presentations with Gamma.app and Canva AI (3 hours)**
- Generating full slide decks from a topic outline using Gamma.app
- Editing AI-generated decks: structure, flow, and visual hierarchy
- Canva AI for quick graphics, infographics, and course thumbnails
- Best practices: when AI-generated visuals work, when they don't
- Hands-on: Each participant creates a 10-slide presentation on their subject

**Module 6: AI for Student Communication and Feedback (2 hours)**
- Writing clear, constructive feedback on student assignments using AI
- Drafting student announcements, emails, and circulars
- Building FAQ documents for common student questions
- Academic integrity: how to set boundaries with AI tool usage in assessments

**Module 7: Building Your Personal AI Workflow (2 hours)**
- Mapping repetitive tasks in your current workflow
- Identifying which tools fit which tasks
- Setting up a personal prompt library
- Time tracking exercise: before and after AI-assisted prep
- Hands-on: Each participant documents their personal AI toolkit

**Module 8: Capstone and Showcase (1 hour)**
- Participants share one AI-created artifact from the 2 days
- Group discussion: departmental adoption strategies
- Q&A and tool troubleshooting session
